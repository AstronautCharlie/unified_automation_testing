  ---
  Methodology

  The file contains raw multi-modal sensor data (no activity labels) from 4
  subjects (S1–S4) across 20 recordings (5 ADL sessions each). The sampling rate
   is ~30 Hz (~33 ms/sample). Classification was done in three steps:

  Step 1 — Identify the "upright" gravity axis

  The back-mounted IMU accelerometer (InertialMeasurementUnit_BACK_acc{X,Y,Z})
  has units of mg (~1000 mg = 1g). Across all subjects, the X axis has a mean of
   ~−870 mg (≈ −1g), while Y and Z are near zero. This confirms that the
  sensor's X-axis aligns with gravity when the person is standing upright.

  Step 2 — Compute trunk tilt angle

  For each sample:

  tilt_angle = arccos(−accX / |acc|)

  - Tilt ≈ 0° → back is vertical (upright: standing or walking)
  - Tilt ≈ 90° → back is horizontal (lying)

  Thresholds chosen:

  ┌─────────────────────┬────────────┐
  │       Posture       │ Tilt range │
  ├─────────────────────┼────────────┤
  │ Standing or walking │ < 30°      │
  ├─────────────────────┼────────────┤
  │ Sitting             │ 30° – 70°  │
  ├─────────────────────┼────────────┤
  │ Lying               │ ≥ 70°      │
  └─────────────────────┴────────────┘

  These thresholds map to realistic trunk inclinations: sitting involves
  moderate forward/backward lean, lying is nearly horizontal.

  Step 3 — Distinguish standing vs. walking

  For upright samples (tilt < 30°), walking is distinguished from standing using
   the 1-second rolling standard deviation of the left-shoe navigation-frame
  acceleration magnitude (L-SHOE Nav_A{x,y,z}). Walking produces periodic,
  high-amplitude foot accelerations; standing is quasi-static.

  Threshold: shoe_acc_std > 150 mg → walking; otherwise → standing

  The shoe std distribution is bimodal (median 39 mg; 75th percentile 669 mg),
  making 150 mg a natural separator.

  Step 4 — Aggregate time

  Number of samples in each posture × 0.033 s per sample, converted to minutes.
