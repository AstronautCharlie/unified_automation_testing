  ---
  Method

  - Loaded all 20 ADL parquet files (644,635 rows total, 242 sensor columns)
  - For each activity column: filtered to labeled rows only, imputed missing
  sensor values with column medians
  - Trained a multiclass Random Forest (overall importance) + one-vs-rest binary
   RF per class (class-specific importance)
  - Reported top 10 sensors per class by feature importance score

  ---
  Cross-cutting patterns

  1. Object accelerometers are the strongest signal when objects carry sensors
  (cup, milk, cheese, knife, water, dishwasher, fridge) — importance scores
  0.05–0.11 vs. ~0.02–0.04 for body sensors
  2. Location tags dominate spatial activities (moving to door, fridge area) —
  LOCATION_TAG1_X and TAG4_X appear in nearly every high-level activity and
  object interaction
  3. IMU magnetometers (not accelerometers) matter most for arm activities —
  magnetic field near metal objects/fixtures gives positional context that pure
  accelerometry can't
  4. Shoe IMU angular velocity is unique to Walk — no other class uses shoe
  sensors strongly
  5. Back/hip accelerometers identify posture (Lie vs. Sit vs. Stand, bending
  for lower drawers)
  6. Gyroscopes on upper/lower arm identify discrete gestures (reach, release,
  stir) while accelerometers identify gross postures
