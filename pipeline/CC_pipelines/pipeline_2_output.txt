  ---
  Time Each Subject Spends in Each Posture

  Method

  Since ADL_no_label.parquet contains no posture labels, postures are inferred
  from raw sensor signals using two steps:

  Step 1 — Posture classification (Lying / Sitting / Upright) via the back IMU 
  accelerometer (InertialMeasurementUnit_BACK_acc{X,Y,Z}):
  - At rest, the accelerometer primarily measures gravity. The BACK sensor's
  X-axis is aligned close to vertical when upright (mean ≈ −861 out of ~1000 =
  1g).
  - We apply a 1-second rolling mean (30 samples at 30 Hz) to isolate the
  gravity component, then compute the tilt angle = arccos(|accX_smooth| /
  magnitude).
    - Tilt < 30° → Upright (standing or walking)
    - Tilt 30–60° → Sitting
    - Tilt > 60° → Lying

  Step 2 — Walking vs. Standing (within Upright):
  - Foot rotation during walking creates large periodic angular velocity in the
  shoe IMUs (L-SHOE and R-SHOE AngVelBodyFrame).
  - We compute the combined shoe angular velocity magnitude and its 1-second
  rolling standard deviation.
    - Rolling std > 800 → Walking; otherwise → Standing.

  Time per sample = 1/30 s (30 Hz), converted to minutes.

  ---
  Key observations

  - S1 and S2 have the most recorded time (~95–100 min). Standing dominates,
  followed closely by walking; they spend relatively little time sitting (~8–13
  min) or lying (~6–7 min).
  - S3 and S4 have shorter recordings (~81 min). They spend proportionally more
  time sitting (~11–16 min) and lying (~10–11 min) versus S1/S2.
  - Lying is rare across all subjects (6–11 min), consistent with kitchen-based
  ADL tasks where subjects are mostly upright.
  - S3 is the only subject where walking time (28.1 min) exceeds standing time
  (25.9 min).

  Caveats

  These are estimates from rule-based heuristics, not ground-truth labels. The
  main sources of error:
  1. Brief forward bends (e.g., reaching into low drawers) may be misclassified
  as "Sitting" since they tilt the back sensor.
  2. The walking threshold (shoe std > 800) may miss slow walks or catch fast
  arm movements.
  3. Rows with missing back IMU data are forward-filled before classification.
