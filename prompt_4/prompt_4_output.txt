  ---
  How to detect arm actions from sensor data

  Primary sensor: lower-arm IMU gyroscope magnitude

  Columns: InertialMeasurementUnit_LLA_gyroX/Y/Z (left),
  InertialMeasurementUnit_RLA_gyroX/Y/Z (right)
  Feature: gyro_mag = √(gyroX² + gyroY² + gyroZ²) in deg/s

  This single scalar is the best discriminator of arm action. The intuition:
  different arm actions involve fundamentally different amounts of arm rotation,
   so the total angular velocity at the forearm cleanly separates them.

  ---
  Action tiers by gyroscope magnitude

  Tier: Very low
  Actions: sip, bite, stir (R)
  LA_gyro range: < 350 deg/s
  Physical signature: Arm held nearly still. sip/bite: arm raised to mouth,
    forearm stationary. stir: small circular wrist motion only.
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  Tier: Low-medium
  Actions: cut, spread (R), move
  LA_gyro range: 350–700
  Physical signature: cut: short push-pull knife strokes, mainly forearm.
  spread:
    lateral wrist strokes. move: whole arm sweeping in transport.
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  Tier: Medium-high
  Actions: open, close
  LA_gyro range: 700–1100
  Physical signature: Door/drawer/fridge manipulation: the whole arm rotates
  from
    the shoulder to pull or push.
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  Tier: High
  Actions: reach, release, lock/unlock, clean
  LA_gyro range: > 1100
  Physical signature: reach: ballistic full-arm swing to grasp. release: arm
    retracts quickly. lock/unlock: intense forearm twist. clean: vigorous
    sustained scrubbing — highest values in dataset (>2000).

  Secondary discriminators

  Gyroscope axis dominance (fraction of total gyro per axis) refines within
  tiers:
  - lock/unlock: X-axis dominant (fracX > 0.45) — forearm supination/pronation
  (turning a key)
  - clean: Y-axis dominant (fracY > 0.43) — lateral back-and-forth scrubbing
  - stir (right arm): X-axis dominant (fracX > 0.49) — circular wrist rotation

  Hand accelerometer Y-axis encodes arm posture via gravity:
  - LH_accY < −300 or RH_accY < −600 → arm elevated toward face → sip or bite
  - LH_accY > +400 → arm extended forward → reach, open, close, clean

  Object sensors confirm the manipulated object:
  - Reed switch state change (1→0 = open; 0→1 = close) during medium-high gyro
  - Cup/glass accelerometer elevated → sip; knife accelerometer elevated →
  cut/spread; spoon → stir

  ---
  Left vs. Right arm asymmetry

  Because subjects are predominantly right-handed:

  - Right arm has higher gyro for nearly every shared action — it's the active
  hand; the left stabilizes.
  - Right arm has exclusive actions: stir and spread — fine-motor dominant-hand
  tasks performed while the left holds the container or food.
  - Bite/sip: Left arm has much lower gyro than right. The right hand raises
  food/cup to the mouth; the left holds the remaining portion still.
  - Clean and reach: Left arm nearly matches right — these require vigorous or
  large bilateral engagement.
